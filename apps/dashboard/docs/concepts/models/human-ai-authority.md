# Human and AI authority

Status: **Draft**

AI may inspect, reason, propose, simulate, and execute actions already allowed
by effective Policy. AI cannot expand its own authority, approve its own
governance changes, conceal provenance, impersonate a human, or mutate terminal
History.

Human approval is required when resolved Policy demands it and for unresolved
model-boundary, authority-expansion, destructive, security-sensitive, or
material production decisions. Human approval also cannot override a deny or
authority the system does not possess.

Every proposal records proposer, rationale, affected definitions, diff,
validation, risk, and expiry. Every decision records actor, scope, exact
approved content/hash, time, and reason. Material changes after approval require
new approval.

Automation may create Draft proposals and low-risk Runs within Policy. Model
meaning changes require explicit owner review before documentation becomes
Canonical.

Open decisions: owner roles, approval thresholds, emergency authority,
delegation, expiry, and appeal/audit process.
